CRAP Website Evaluations

Contrast, Repitition, Alignment, Proximity

Lenovo

  • Contrast - Sections alternate between pictures and descriptive text, making it clear where each section ends and begins with contrast.
  • Repitition - Sections with different products and topics are repeated, alternating between photos and text-only descriptions that provide contrast as previously mentioned.
  • Alignment - The images, text, and icons on the Lenovo homepage are neatly aligned. Even when there are different numbers of icons on neighboring lines(ie, 3 photos above 4 text blocks), the alignment does not appear messy. The website is clean and all of the elements work well with each other.
  • Proximity - The website does not feel crowded or hard to parse. Elements are arranged neatly, but not too close or too far away from each other.

Yale School of Art

  • Contrast - Many portions of this website are black colors against neon backgrounds that are somewhat difficult to read. Other sections use text against a photo background, making it more difficult to read.
  • Repitition - Very few things are repeated on this page. Each element is different from the others, besides several neon yellow to white gradient text boxes.
  • Alignment - The content of this website is mostly on the left-most side of the page. Individual elements are often out of line with neighboring elements, and some transparent yellow boxes are randomly places throughout the page without being aligned to any element.
  • Proximity - Half of this page is crowded, while the other half is empty, filled only with the tiled image background of a chair and stool. Elements are not consistently spaced out, and there are many different size gaps between text boxes.
